There are certain questions that I find myself asking on a fairly regular basis. What should I do in the future? What should I do right now? How should what I need do in the future affect what I should do now? What do I even want to do? Is what I want to do truly meaningful? What does it mean for something to be meaningful? Though I am lucky enough to currently be in a position of relative comfort, I can imagine going through horrible events that also make me question what the meaning of everything is. These questions that tend to linger on one's mind often boil down to questions about *meaning*.

To assert that life is meaningless gives the impression of a depressed, nihilistic social pariah while asserting that life is full of meaning gives the impression of a privileged brat that is blissfully unaware of the poverty, war, disaster, and social injustice that pervades our world. And yet, it seems to me that anyone not blind to our absurd, tragic, incomprehensible reality has asked themselves the question of meaning.

Western society has largely embraced the idea of subjective meaning. We respect how people choose to live their lives and dare not say that their lives are less meaningful than ours. Mainstream media is filled with messages about choosing your own destiny, forging your own path, or being your authentic self.
