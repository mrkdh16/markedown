There are certain questions that I find myself asking on a fairly regular basis. What should I do in the future? What should I do right now? How should what I need do in the future affect what I should do now? What do I even want to do? Is what I want to do truly meaningful? What does it mean for something to be meaningful? Though I am lucky enough to currently be in a position of relative comfort, I can imagine going through horrible events that also make me question what the meaning of everything is. These questions that tend to linger on one's mind often boil down to questions about *meaning*.

In [[Man's Search for Meaning]], Viktor Frankl describes the existential vacuum as an experience of *inner emptiness* which is caused by a *lack of awareness of a meaning worth living for*. The existential vacuum does not necessarily have a single, intelligible cause. It can build slowly over time due to a variety of traumatic experiences or hit you all at once for seemingly no reason at all on a random Sunday. Keeping oneself busy can seem like a solution but it merely reduces the frequency with which the inner emptiness is felt. It can become hard to shake the feeling that one is living in denial of the reality of meaninglessness.
