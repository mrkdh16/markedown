For those that don't subscribe to a theistic worldview, we must explain our reasoning capabilities through an evolutionary process. A potential issue with this is that evolution has no reason to optimize for truth-seeking ability. Suppose there were certain truisms independent of the material world. Evolution has no reason to develop beings that have the capability of detecting these truisms. This throws into question the validity of our reasoning capabilities. How can we trust our brains to know what is true? 

For simplicity, let's first throw out the metaphysical contraband that is objective truth, i.e. let's assume there isn't some immaterial realm of objective truth that exists independent of the material world that we interact with. Evolution clearly has reason to create brains that are suited for well modeling the environment around it. In addition, these brains need not have the ability to grasp any notion of truth to simply observe and generate predictions about their environment. It certainly seems convenient to trust our reasoning capabilities when it starts to generate shockingly accurate predictions of the world around us.
