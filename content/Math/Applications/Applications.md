I believe that every mathematical idea can and will eventually find an application in the real world, whether it's in physics, computer science, engineering, or even biology. Many great thinkers have alluded to [[The Unreasonable Effectiveness of Mathematics in the Natural Sciences]] and I believe that this is because much of the universe is fundamentally coherent (for whatever reason). The level of abstraction that mathematics deals with is its greatest strength and is why abstract, seemingly useless math can find surprising applications.

---
The [[Singular Value Decomposition]] (SVD) allows us to break apart matrices and see what vectors matter (statistically) the most. If the SVD enables us to compress dense matrices, [[Compressed Sensing]] enables us to reconstruct dense matrices from sparse measurements.

Through [[Markov Chains]], we can effectively model sequential probabilistic phenomena.

[[Linear Regression and Kernel Methods]] are under-appreciated statistical learning algorithms that are quite powerful and contain fascinating math.
