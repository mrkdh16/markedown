## Why Philosophy?
Behind every decision we make, there is inevitably a set of values (in the most broad sense of value) that consciously or unconsciously drives us. Even a mundane decision such as eating food is motivated by something. Perhaps there is a belief that one's life has inherent value and is worth preserving, or a more instinctive belief that eating that food will lead to pleasure and a possibility for more pleasure. The latter places value on pleasure. Humans seem to be unique in that we have the capacity to choose the values that drive us. We have the choice to consciously shape the way we make decisions. 

To me, the art of philosophy lies in discovering or inventing one's own value system. I believe in constructing a coherent value system using intuition and reason. I will admit, I have no good justification for this belief other than the fact that I enjoy the construction process. An increased sense of fulfillment and meaning is an added bonus.
