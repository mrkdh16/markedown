I saw this symbol for the first time when I took classical mechanics in my first year. As a math major, I was appalled and disgusted. I kind of understood what the professor wanted to get across, but I couldn't believe that he would offhandedly write such an egregious thing and not even provide a definition. My physics major friend sitting next to me couldn't stop laughing at my reaction and the 'approximately greater than' sign soon became a running joke in our friend group about how handwavey physicists are.

Now that I'm working at a lab full of physicists, I've come to appreciate the ethos that this symbol conveys—the philosophy of intuition over rigor.
