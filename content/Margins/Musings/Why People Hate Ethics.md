## What is the right thing to do? 
Seems like a simple enough question. Is there a right answer? Suppose there is a right answer and morality is objective. Now the problem becomes is: how do we justify our belief in objective morality? Is strong intuition enough to justify our moral beliefs? Historically, intuition has failed us time and time again. There turned out to be a good explanation for lightning, rainbows, earthquakes, diseases, the Sun rising and falling, and the diverse array of beautiful animals and plants that populate our planet. While moral truths certainly seem to have a different quality than physical truths, the skeptic is led to question our moral intuition as well. We humans are also subject to all sorts of biases including in-group bias, survivorship bias, confirmation bias, hindsight bias, selection bias, and more. There is good reason to believe that these biases affect our moral judgement. Furthermore, we know that different cultures have different moral intuitions and these moral intuitions have changed over time. Using intuition as a moral compass is starting to seem more and more dicey.
## Constructing Morality
Ok, so it seems that we can't be trusted to intuit objective moral truths. The reasonable response is to use reason to guide our moral intuition. Now that reason has entered the picture, we can construct moral value systems that definitively tell us which actions are right. How should we construct such a system? There already exist numerous value systems including but not limited to: utilitarianism, contractualism, deontology, virtue ethics, and Confucianism. How are we to choose? Ultimately, we have no choice but to use our intuition which we already agreed not to trust. Although, this time we used reason to try to mitigate bias and get to what is actually right. Suppose we chose a coherent system that feels right. We may run into the problem where our system seems to justify actions that seem intuitively wrong to us: e.g. utilitarianism may justify torture as a means to an end. Do we bite the bullet and say that our intuition is wrong? Do we adjust our system at the cost of consistency? Do we change systems entirely?
## Moral Relativism
So, perhaps there is no 'right' moral value system. We also know from experience that different people hold different values. Maybe we all get to choose our own system based on what we hold near and dear. What happens when a masochist chooses to value inflicting pain? It seems that we shouldn't just let this person go on a rampage. How are we to convince this person when there is no right moral value system? Are we justified in using force to stop this person? What does it even mean to be justified in doing something? 


## Losing Our Humanity
