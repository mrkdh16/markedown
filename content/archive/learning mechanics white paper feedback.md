Analogies.
- "*Where mechanistic interpretability aims to be the biology of deep learning, learning mechanics aspires to be its physics, mirroring the complementary relationship between biology and physics in the natural sciences.*"
- "*The radical engineering success of scaling neural networks suggests some deep underlying scientific principles which we have not found. This has historically been the case when technology has surpassed science; for example, practical problems with steam engines largely motivated thermodynamics, which went on to explain much more than engine efficiency. In our case, the same scientific principles behind neural networks may underlie our own intelligence and have major implications for neuroscience.*"

Punchy one-liners
- *"It is also worth emphasizing that this theory of deep learning will not — and should not — aim to describe everything. A map at the full resolution of the world would be the size of the world and thus of little use. What we seek instead is a theory that operates at the right level of resolution — one that sacrifices detail in favor of insight."*
	- To me, this is the philosophy at the heart of learning mechanics that sets it apart from other deep learning theory; the emphasis on intuition, not technical complexity
- "*The central challenge is not opacity, but complexity. While we have direct access to the architecture, data, task, and learning rule, the interaction of these components gives rise to learning dynamics that are nonlinear, coupled, and high-dimensional.*"

The five lines of evidence.
1. there exist settings in which the learning dynamics can be analytically solved; 
2. insightful limits can reveal fundamental learning phenomena; 
3. macroscopic observables can be well-captured by simple equations; 
4. hyperparameters can be disentangled and understood independently; and 
5. deep learning increasingly shows universal behavior across systems and settings.

Seven desiderata for a scientific theory of deep learning.
1. It should be **mathematical**, making unambiguous quantitative statements about important properties of neural networks. 
2. It should be **predictive**, making claims supported by simple, repeatable empirical measurements. 
3. It should be **fundamental**, proceeding logically from a first-principles description of neural network training. 
4. It should be **humble**, being solid in what it describes and clear-eyed about what it cannot. 
5. It should be **intuitive**, being simple, illuminating, and satisfying in its demystification of deep learning. 
6. It should be **comprehensive**, describing aspects of neural networks’ training process, hidden representations, and final weights in a single unified picture. 
7. Finally, it should be **useful**, serving as the scientific foundation for applied deep learning as physics does for other forms of engineering.
