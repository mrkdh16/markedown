## I. Binary Operations, Closures, and Isomorphisms (Lecture 1)

### Definitions
1. **Binary Operation**: A function $*: S \times S \to S$, written $a * b$. It must be defined for all $a, b \in S$ and the result must be in $S$.
2. **Closed Subset**: A subset $H \subseteq S$ is **closed** under $_$ if for $h_1, h_2 \in H$, $h_1 * h_2 \in H$. In this case, $_$ induces a binary operation on $H$.
3. **Commutative Operation**: An operation $*$ on $S$ is **commutative** if $a * b = b * a$ for all $a, b \in S$.
4. **Associative Operation**: An operation $*$ on $S$ is **associative** if $a * (b * c) = (a * b) * c$ for all $a, b, c \in S$.
5. **Binary Algebraic Structure**: A structure $(S, _)$, where $_$ is a binary operation on $S$.
6. **Identity Element**: An element $e \in S$ is an **identity element** if $e * s = s * e = s$ for all $s \in S$.
7. **Isomorphism (Binary Structures)**: An isomorphism of $(S, *)$ with $(S', *')$ is a **one-to-one and onto function (bijection)** $\phi: S \to S'$ such that the **homomorphism property** holds: $\phi(x * y) = \phi(x) *' \phi(y)$ for all $x, y \in S$. If such a map exists, $(S, *)$ and $(S', *')$ are "isomorphic," denoted $(S, *) \cong (S', *')$.
### Theorems & Propositions
- **Theorem (Identity Uniqueness)**: If $(S, *)$ has an identity element, then **it is unique**.
- **Theorem (Isomorphism Preserves Identity)**: If $\phi: S \to S'$ is an isomorphism and $e$ is an identity element of $S$, then **$\phi(e)$ is an identity in $S'$**.
## II. Groups and Subgroups (Lectures 2, 3, 5)
### Definitions
1. **Group**: A binary algebraic structure $\langle G, *\rangle$ satisfying three axioms:
    - (1) **Associativity**: $a * (b * c) = (a * b) * c$ for all $a, b, c \in G$.
    - (2) **Identity**: There is an identity element $e \in G$ such that $e * a = a * e = a$ for all $a \in G$.
    - (3) **Inverse**: Each $a \in G$ has an inverse $a^{-1} \in G$ such that $a * a^{-1} = a^{-1} * a = e$.
2. **Abelian Group**: A group $G$ is **abelian** if the operation $*$ is commutative ($a * b = b * a$ for all $a, b \in G$).
3. **Subgroup**: A subset $H$ of a group $G$ is a **subgroup** ($H \le G$) if it is closed under the operation $*$ of $G$, such that $\langle H, *\rangle$ is itself a group.
4. **Cyclic Subgroup** ($\langle a \rangle$): For an element $a$ of a group $G$, the **cyclic subgroup generated by $a$** is $H = {a^n | n \in \mathbb{Z}}$.
5. **Cyclic Group**: A group $G$ is **cyclic** if there is some element $a$ that generates it, i.e., $\langle a \rangle = G$.
6. **Subgroup Generated by a Set** ($\langle a_i | i \in I \rangle$): The **subgroup generated by ${a_i | i \in I}$** is the intersection of all subgroups $H \le G$ containing all $a_i$. This is the smallest subgroup of $G$ containing all $a_i$.
7. **Finitely Generated Group**: $G$ is **finitely generated** if there exists a finite set ${a_1, \dots, a_n}$ such that $\langle a_1, \dots, a_n \rangle = G$.
### Theorems & Corollaries (Group Properties and Subgroup Tests)
- **Theorem (Cancellation Law for Groups)**: For a group $\langle G, *\rangle$, if $a * b = a * c$, then $b = c$.
- **Theorem (Unique Solution)**: For $a, b \in G$, $a * X = b$ has a **unique solution** $X$ in $G$.
- **Theorem (Unique Inverse)**: An element $a$ in a group $\langle G, *\rangle$ has a **unique inverse**.
- **Corollary (Inverse of a Product)**: For $a, b \in G$, $(a * b)^{-1} = b^{-1} * a^{-1}$.
- **Theorem (Subgroup Test)**: A subset $H$ of $G$ is a subgroup if:
    - (1) $H$ is **closed** under the operation of $G$.
    - (2) The **identity element $e$** of $G$ is in $H$.
    - (3) For any $a \in H$, the **inverse $a^{-1}$** is also in $H$.
- **Theorem (Intersection of Subgroups)**: If $H_i \le G$ for $i \in I$, then their intersection $K = \bigcap_{i \in I} H_i$ is also a subgroup of $G$ ($K \le G$).
- **Proposition** (Property of Generated Subgroup): $\langle a, b \rangle$ is a subgroup of $G$.
## III. Cyclic Groups, Cosets, and Lagrange's Theorem (Lectures 4, 8)
### Definitions
1. **Relatively Prime**: Integers $r, s$ are **relatively prime** if $\text{gcd}(r, s) = 1$.
2. **Left/Right Cosets**: For $H \le G$ and $a \in G$:
    - **Left coset**: $aH = {ah | h \in H}$.
    - **Right coset**: $Ha = {ha | h \in H}$.
3. **Index** ($G:H$): The number of left cosets of $H$ in $G$. If $G$ is finite, $(G:H) = |G|/|H|$.
### Lemmas, Theorems & Corollaries (Cyclic Groups)
- **Theorem (Cyclic Groups are Abelian)**: A cyclic group is abelian.
- **Theorem (Division Algorithm)**: If $n, m \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $m > 0$, there exist unique $q$ (quotient) and $r$ (remainder) such that $n = mq + r$ and $0 \le r < m$.
- **Theorem (Subgroups of Cyclic Groups)**: Any subgroup of a cyclic group is **cyclic**.
- **Corollary (Subgroups of $\mathbb{Z}$)**: The subgroups of $\langle \mathbb{Z}, +\rangle$ are precisely the groups $n\mathbb{Z}$ for $n \in \mathbb{Z}$.
- **Theorem (Classification of Cyclic Groups)**: Let $G = \langle a \rangle$ be a cyclic group.
    - (1) If $G$ is **infinite**, then $G \cong \langle \mathbb{Z}, +\rangle$.
    - (2) If $G$ is **finite of order $n$**, then $G \cong \langle \mathbb{Z}_n, +\rangle$.
- **Theorem (Order of Subgroups in $\mathbb{Z}_n$)**: Let $G = \langle a \rangle$ be a cyclic group of order $n$. The subgroup $\langle a^s \rangle$ has order **$n/\text{gcd}(s, n)$**.
- **Corollary (Generators of $\mathbb{Z}_n$)**: The elements $a^s$ that generate $G = \langle a \rangle$ (order $n$) are those where $s$ and $n$ are **relatively prime** ($\text{gcd}(s, n) = 1$).
### Theorems & Corollaries (Cosets and Orders)
- **Proposition (Coset Size)**: Every (left or right) coset of $H \le G$ has the **same number of elements as $H$**.
- **Theorem (Lagrange's Theorem)**: Let $G$ be a **finite group** and $H \le G$. Then **$|H|$ divides $|G|$**.
- **Corollary (Prime Order Groups)**: Every group of prime order $p$ is **cyclic** and therefore **abelian**.
- **Corollary (Order of an Element)**: The order of an element $a \in G$ (which is $|\langle a \rangle|$) divides the order of the group $|G|$.
- **Corollary (Element Exponentiation)**: If $G$ is a finite group of order $n$ and $a \in G$, then **$a^n = e$**.
- **Theorem (Fermat's Little Theorem)**: Let $p$ be prime and $a \in {1, \dots, p-1}$. Then $a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$. (This follows because the order of $a$ in $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ divides $p-1$).
## IV. Permutation Groups and Actions (Lectures 6, 7, 12)
### Definitions
1. **Permutation**: A one-to-one and onto function (bijection) $A \to A$.
2. **Symmetric Group ($S_A$ or $S_n$)**: The set of all permutations of $A$, denoted $S_A$. If $A = {1, \dots, n}$, $S_n$ is the symmetric group on $n$ letters, and $|S_n| = n!$.
3. **Dihedral Group ($D_n$)**: The group of symmetries of a regular $n$-gon. $|D_n| = 2n$.
4. **Transposition**: A permutation that swaps two elements (a 2-cycle).
5. **Even/Odd Permutation**: A permutation is **even** or **odd** according to whether it can be expressed as a product of an even or odd number of transpositions.
6. **Alternating Group ($A_n$)**: The set of all even permutations, which forms a subgroup of $S_n$.
7. **Group Action**: A map $*: G \times X \to X$ (written $g * x$) satisfying: (1) $e * x = x$ and (2) $(g_1 g_2) * x = g_1 * (g_2 * x)$. $X$ is a **$G$-set**.
8. **Faithful Action**: $G$ acts **faithfully** on $X$ if the induced homomorphism $\phi: G \to S_X$ is injective. Equivalently, if $g * x = x$ for all $x \in X$, then $g = e$.
9. **Transitive Action**: $G$ acts **transitively** on $X$ if for any $x_1, x_2 \in X$, there exists $g \in G$ with $g x_1 = x_2$.
10. **Stabilizer (Isotropy) Subgroup** ($G_x$): For $x \in X$, $G_x = {g \in G | g * x = x}$.
11. **Orbit** ($G x$): For $x \in X$, the cell containing $x$ under the equivalence relation defined by the action, $G x = {g x | g \in G}$.
### Theorems & Propositions
- **Theorem (Symmetric Group Structure)**: $S_A$ (or $S_n$) is a group under permutation multiplication.
- **Theorem (Cayley's Theorem)**: Every group $G$ is **isomorphic to a subgroup of $S_G$** (the group of permutations of $G$).
- **Theorem (Cycle Decomposition)**: Every permutation $\sigma$ of a finite set has a **unique decomposition as a product of disjoint cycles** (up to commuting the cycles).
- **Theorem (Parity Theorem)**: No permutation in $S_n$ can be expressed both as a product of an **even number** of transpositions and as an **odd number**.
- **Theorem (Alternating Group)**: $A_n$ is a subgroup of $S_n$ and has order **$|A_n| = |S_n|/2$** for $n \ge 2$.
- **Theorem (Action Induces Homomorphism)**: Let $X$ be a $G$-set. The function $\phi: G \to S_X$ defined by $\phi(g) = \sigma_g$ (where $\sigma_g(x) = g x$) is a **group homomorphism**.
- **Proposition (Stabilizer is a Subgroup)**: For any $x \in X$, the stabilizer $G_x$ is a **subgroup of $G$**.
- **Theorem (Orbit-Stabilizer Theorem)**: Let $X$ be a $G$-set and $x \in X$. Then the size of the orbit is equal to the index of the stabilizer: **$|G x| = (G : G_x)$**.
## V. Homomorphisms, Normal Subgroups, and Quotient Groups (Lectures 10, 11, 13)
### Definitions
1. **Homomorphism**: A map $\phi: G \to G'$ is a **homomorphism** if $\phi(ab) = \phi(a)\phi(b)$ for all $a, b \in G$.
2. **Kernel** ($\text{Ker}(\phi)$): For a homomorphism $\phi: G \to G'$, the kernel is the subgroup $\text{Ker}(\phi) = {g \in G | \phi(g) = e'}$, where $e'$ is the identity in $G'$.
3. **Normal Subgroup** ($H \triangleleft G$): A subgroup $H \le G$ is **normal** if $g H = H g$ for all $g \in G$.
4. **Quotient Group ($G/H$)**: If $H$ is a normal subgroup of $G$, $G/H$ is the group formed by the set of left cosets ${aH | a \in G}$ under the operation $(aH) * (bH) = (ab)H$.
5. **Automorphism**: An **isomorphism from $G$ to $G$**.
6. **Conjugate**: For $x, g \in G$, $g x g^{-1}$ is the **conjugate** of $x$ by $g$.
7. **Inner Automorphism**: An automorphism $I_g: G \to G$ defined by $I_g(x) = g x g^{-1}$ for a fixed $g \in G$.
8. **Center** ($Z(G)$): $Z(G) = {z \in G | z g = g z, \forall g \in G}$.
9. **Simple Group**: A group $G$ is **simple** if it has no nontrivial proper normal subgroups.
10. **Maximal Normal Subgroup**: A normal subgroup $M \triangleleft G$ such that there is no proper normal subgroup $N$ of $G$ with $M \subsetneq N \subsetneq G$.
### Theorems & Propositions
- **Proposition (Homomorphism Preserves Abelian Property)**: If $\phi: G \to G'$ is a homomorphism **onto** $G'$, and $G$ is abelian, then $G'$ is abelian.
- **Theorem (Homomorphism Properties)**: If $\phi: G \to G'$ is a homomorphism:
    - (1) $\phi(e) = e'$.
    - (2) $\phi(a^{-1}) = \phi(a)^{-1}$.
    - (3) If $H \le G$, then the image **$\phi[H] \le G'$** (is a subgroup).
    - (4) If $K' \le \phi[G]$, then the inverse image **$\phi^{-1}[K'] \le G$** (is a subgroup).
- **Theorem (Kernel is Normal)**: $\text{Ker}(\phi)$ is a **normal subgroup** of $G$.
- **Theorem (Injectivity and Kernel)**: A homomorphism $\phi: G \to G'$ is **one-to-one** if and only if **$\text{Ker}(\phi) = {e}$**.
- **Proposition (Quotient Operation)**: The operation $(aH) * (bH) = (ab)H$ on $G/H$ is **well-defined if and only if $H$ is normal in $G$**.
- **Theorem (Natural Homomorphism)**: Let $H \triangleleft G$. The map $v: G \to G/H$ given by $v(x) = xH$ is a homomorphism with **$\text{Ker}(v) = H$**.
- **Theorem (Fundamental Homomorphism Theorem)**: Let $\phi: G \to G'$ be a homomorphism with $\text{Kernel } H$. Then $\phi[G]$ is a group and the map $\mu: G/H \to \phi[G]$ given by $\mu(gH) = \phi(g)$ is an **isomorphism**.
- **Proposition (Center is Normal)**: $Z(G)$ is a **normal subgroup** of $G$.
- **Proposition (Commutator Subgroup is Normal)**: The commutator subgroup $C$ is a **normal subgroup** of $G$.
- **Theorem (Maximal Normal Subgroups and Simple Quotients)**: $M$ is a maximal normal subgroup of $G$ if and only if **$G/M$ is a simple group**.
- **Theorem (Simple Abelian Groups)**: $\mathbb{Z}_n$ is simple if and only if **$n$ is prime**. These are the only simple abelian groups.
- **Theorem (Alternating Group Simplicity)**: The alternating group $A_n$ is **simple for $n \ge 5$**.
- **Claim/Fact (Index 2 Implies Normal)**: If $(G:H) = 2$, then **$H$ is normal in $G$**.
## VI. Direct Products and Finitely Generated Abelian Groups (Lecture 9)
### Definitions
1. **Direct Pro duct**: For groups $G_1, \dots, G_n$, the direct product $G_1 \times \dots \times G_n$ is a group under the operation $(a_1, \dots, a_n) * (b_1, \dots, b_n) = (a_1 b_1, \dots, a_n b_n)$.
### Theorems
- **Theorem (Order in Direct Product)**: Let $(a_1, \dots, a_n) \in G_1 \times \dots \times G_n$. If each $a_i$ has finite order $r_i$ in $G_i$, the element $(a_1, \dots, a_n)$ has order **$\text{lcm}(r_1, \dots, r_n)$** (the least common multiple of the individual orders).
- **Theorem (Cyclic Direct Product)**: $\mathbb{Z}_m \times \mathbb{Z}_n$ is cyclic (and thus isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_{mn}$) if and only if $m$ and $n$ are **relatively prime** ($\text{gcd}(m, n) = 1$).
- **Corollary (Generalized Cyclic Direct Product)**: The group $\mathbb{Z}_{m_1} \times \dots \times \mathbb{Z}_{m_k}$ is cyclic if $\text{gcd}(m_i, m_j) = 1$ for all $i \ne j$.
- **Theorem (Fundamental Theorem of Finitely Generated Abelian Groups - FTFGAG)**: Every finitely generated abelian group $G$ is isomorphic to a direct product of the form $\mathbb{Z}_{p_1^{r_1}} \times \dots \times \mathbb{Z}_{p_k^{r_k}} \times \mathbb{Z} \times \dots \times \mathbb{Z}$, where $p_i$ are primes. This decomposition is **unique** up to permuting factors.
- **Theorem (Converse of Lagrange for Abelian Groups)**: If $m$ divides the order of a finite abelian group $G$, then **$G$ has a subgroup of order $m$**.
- **Theorem (Squarefree Abelian Groups are Cyclic)**: If $m$ is a squarefree integer (not divisible by $p^2$ for any prime $p$), then every abelian group of order $m$ is **cyclic**.
